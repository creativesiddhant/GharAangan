Frequently Asked Questions

Pahadi Ghee is made from the milk of native cow breeds in Uttarakhand that feed on medicinal herbs, shrubs, and pure mineral-rich water in high-altitude pastures. The combination of herb-rich cow diet and the traditional wood-fired Bilona method creates a unique taste, texture, and high therapeutic value.

Unlike modern commercial ghee made by heating milk cream, the traditional Bilona method involves boiling milk, setting it into curd overnight, and then hand-churning the curd with a wooden churner (Bilona) to separate butter (Makhan). This butter is then slowly clarified on a wood fire to obtain pure granular ghee.
